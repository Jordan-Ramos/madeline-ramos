About Me

About

I'm a Furniture major from North Carolina, land of the famed beaches and mountains. Being outdoors hiking and exploring my surroundings makes me feel like I can breathe better and has always influenced my work. Though I began my time at SCAD as an Interior Design major, I quickly fell in love with the physicality of furniture making. Studying the qualities of wood as I watch my idea come to life is ever-gratifying. I love that the trees I grew up playing in have become my livelihood, and though making is a passion of mine, I've become fascinated with the furniture market and trend analysis in the process. If you'd like to discuss the best N.C. beaches, see pictures of my dogs, or have any other business inquiries, you can contact me here!

Furniture

Blushing Surf Coffee Table

Part of a small three-piece collection that was designed around blushed tones and curved forms. This piece embodies a sense of playfulness and an added pop of color.

Frill Ottoman

This fun and perky ottoman was designed to correlate with the Blushing Surf Coffee Table. The upholstery was hand-sewn and fitted to a preexisting ottoman frame.

Trinity Candelabra

Made from 15 gauge round tube steel, this geometric candelabra was MIG welded with 45-degree angles at each corner. It is light and elegant.

Hangmans Light

Made up of mixed materials, this hanging light is also used as a coat rack. This piece is a correlation between molded concrete, 15 gauge square tube, and a weaved cord.

Dimple Lamp

Constructed of only five parts; 3D printed white feet, CNC walnut round base, laser-cut leather, small metal supports, and a lightbulb. It is all things soft and sweet.

Whisk Chair

Designed around the design language of Emeco. The chair set would be made completely out of recycled cosmetic waste, which produces 120 billion units of waste every year. The recycled seat is complemented by the classic look of Emeco's aluminum legs.

Interior

Go Fish Digital

I was the Interior Designer for Go Fish Digital's new and improved office space in downtown Raleigh, NC. I created a floor plan with furniture pieces I selected that would be optimal for their work environment. Some furniture pieces like the community, breakroom, call room, and conference room tables were custom made by me while working at Enkle Designs, a custom Scandinavian furniture design company. I also selected art and decor, and created a one of a kind wall installation that was over 30ft long and comprised of over 100 individual pieces. Everything successfully came together in 3 months and gave the office space a modern industrial feel with a pop of color.

Kavilah

This was a collaboration project between interior and furniture design students. The design intent for this project was to renovate a preexisting property located in Savannah, Georgia to be a new spot for a restaurant. The entirety of the design was based around the name of the restaurant, Kavilah, meaning a stretch of sand in Greek. While the ocean has a fusion of materials, sand, and water, the interior will encompass the same sense of movement and fusion. The mixture between the ocean and the sand provides the perfect example of mixed materials while having a sense of fluidity. The space will provide a relaxing and inviting feeling for clients as they enjoy amazing food and drinks. With the combination of natural materials and soft textures, it combines the feel of a natural outdoor environment within an interior space.
